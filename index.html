Current Focus

Multi-archive scheduling for robust illumination (MAS-QD) and quality-diversity approaches to biological motif discovery.

Research

My work treats optimization as solution space characterization: using QD to discover diverse, high-quality hypotheses and make them interpretable through behavioral descriptors, visualization, and human-in-the-loop interaction.

Core Themes

  • Quality-Diversity algorithms (MAP-Elites variants, emitters, scheduling)
  • Scientific discovery via exploratory search (bioinformatics, behavioral data)
  • Robust illumination across multiple descriptor views (multi-archive methods)
  • Interpretability through archive querying, visualization, and interaction

Methods

  • MAP-Elites, emitter design, archive metrics (coverage, QD-score)
  • Scheduling / meta-control (bandits, adaptive routing)
  • Offline evaluation pipelines and reproducible experimentation
  • Visualization for archive inspection and trade-off analysis
